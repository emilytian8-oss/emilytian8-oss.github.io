Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Below are detailed FAQs for multilayer, HDI, high-frequency, rigid-flex, blind & buried via, impedance-controlled and hybrid-lamination PCBs. For specific projects, please send your files to sales@yudapcb.com.

1. Multilayer PCB

Q1: What basic data do you need to quote a multilayer PCB? +

We need complete Gerber/ODB++ data and key parameters: layer count, stack-up drawing, finished board thickness, finished copper thickness (inner/outer), material type/Tg, surface finish, solder mask and silkscreen color, quantity and required lead time. If there are any special tests (impedance, reliability, etc.), please also indicate them in the fabrication notes.

For multilayer PCB RFQs, please email sales@yudapcb.com, call +86 135 3804 3239, or visit Room 1102, Chengjin C‑PARK, Kexin Road, Dalingshan Town, Dongguan, Guangdong, China.

Q2: What multilayer PCB layer counts and thicknesses can you typically support? +

In general, we fabricate multilayer PCBs from 4 to about 40 layers. Common finished board thicknesses are 0.6–2.0 mm, but thicker boards are possible depending on the stack-up and mechanical requirements. For very high layer counts or unusual thicknesses, our engineering team will review the design and confirm feasibility with you.

For stack-up and thickness confirmation, contact sales@yudapcb.com or +86 135 3804 3239.

Q3: What is your typical trace/space and via capability for multilayer PCBs? +

Typical minimum trace/space for standard multilayer PCBs is around 4/4 mil. Smaller line widths and spacing may be achievable after DFM review. For vias, finished mechanical drill sizes down to about 0.20 mm are standard; 0.15 mm is possible on some designs but requires closer evaluation. We also support laser microvias for HDI structures.

For detailed design rules, please send your design to sales@yudapcb.com.

Q4: Which materials do you usually use for multilayer boards? +

Standard multilayer PCBs are typically built with FR‑4 materials from recognized brands, with Tg values such as Tg135, Tg150 or Tg170 according to your requirements. For applications with higher temperature, higher reliability or special performance, we can also source high‑Tg or low‑loss laminates as requested.

For material selection questions, contact sales@yudapcb.com.

Q5: What surface finishes are available for multilayer PCBs? +

We can offer HASL, lead‑free HASL, ENIG (immersion gold over nickel), OSP and other surface finishes depending on your design and application. ENIG and OSP are often preferred for fine‑pitch, BGA and high‑reliability applications, while HASL/lead‑free HASL is commonly used for cost‑sensitive products.

For finish selection, please email sales@yudapcb.com.

Q6: Can you control impedance on multilayer PCBs and what data do you need? +

Yes, we can control impedance on multilayer PCBs. We need your target impedance (single‑ended and/or differential), a proposed stack‑up, and information about trace width/spacing and which layers the controlled traces are on. We will use our impedance modelling tools to fine‑tune the dimensions and can provide test coupons and reports upon request.

For impedance control inquiries, contact sales@yudapcb.com.

Q7: How do you verify the quality of multilayer PCBs before shipment? +

We perform a combination of electrical testing (100% for batch orders), AOI inspection where applicable, solder mask and surface finish checks, and final visual inspection based on IPC standards. For some projects, we can also perform additional tests such as cross‑sections, reliability coupons, and specific customer‑defined verification.

For quality control details, please email sales@yudapcb.com.

Q8: What documentation do you provide together with multilayer PCB shipments? +

Standard documentation usually includes a packing list, test reports (electrical test), and, if requested, impedance test reports and cross‑section photos. We can also attach certificates of compliance and material certificates where required by your quality system.

For document requirements, contact sales@yudapcb.com.

Q9: Can you help to optimize multilayer stack-up for cost and performance? +

Yes. Based on your performance targets, impedance requirements and cost constraints, our engineers can propose alternative stack‑ups, material combinations and copper distribution to make your design more manufacturable and economical, while still meeting your technical needs.

For stack-up optimization, send your requirements to sales@yudapcb.com.

Q10: What are typical lead times for multilayer prototype and volume production? +

Typical lead times for standard multilayer prototypes are around 5–7 working days, small batches 7–10 working days, and volume production around 10–15 working days. Complex HDI, special materials or very thick boards may require additional time. We also support expedited services after evaluation.

For schedule confirmation or expedite requests, call +86 135 3804 3239 or email sales@yudapcb.com.

Q11: Do you support panelization and V-cut for multilayer PCBs? +

Yes, we can follow your panelization design or help you design a panel according to your assembly process. V‑cut, tab‑routing and combination methods are available. Please indicate panel size limits and assembly requirements when sending your files.

For panelization help, contact sales@yudapcb.com.

Q12: Can you build multilayer PCBs with different copper thicknesses in inner and outer layers? +

Yes. It is common to have different copper thicknesses for inner vs. outer layers (for example 1 oz inner layers and 1.5–2 oz outer layers). Please specify your exact copper thickness requirements in the stack‑up and notes so that we can evaluate the process accordingly.

For copper thickness options, email sales@yudapcb.com.

2. HDI PCB

Q21: What HDI stack-up structures can you manufacture (e.g. 1+N+1, 2+N+2, 3+N+3)? +

We can manufacture common HDI stack‑ups such as 1+N+1, 2+N+2 and 3+N+3. The exact feasible structure depends on your line/space, via dimensions, layer count and reliability requirements. For more complex build‑ups, our engineers will evaluate the design and confirm the process flow with you.

For HDI stack-up discussion, contact sales@yudapcb.com.

Q22: What is your typical laser microvia capability for HDI PCBs? +

Our typical HDI laser microvias are designed with a finished hole size around 0.10–0.15 mm, depending on the layer structure and material. The capture pad and annular ring dimensions should follow our recommended design rules or be agreed upon after DFM review to ensure stable yield.

For detailed microvia rules, email sales@yudapcb.com.

Q23: Do you support stacked microvias and via-in-pad for HDI designs? +

Yes, we can support both stacked and staggered microvias, as well as via‑in‑pad designs for BGAs and fine‑pitch components. Stacked via structures and via‑in‑pad generally require more careful process control and may have stricter design limits, so our engineering team will evaluate and confirm the best approach.

For via-in-pad HDI designs, contact sales@yudapcb.com.

Q24: What information should I include in my HDI PCB drawings to avoid misunderstanding? +

Please include a clear stack‑up diagram, via layer definitions (which layers are connected by blind/ buried vias), whether vias are stacked or staggered, any via‑in‑pad locations, minimum line/space and spacing requirements in HDI areas, and any special reliability or test requirements.

For HDI documentation review, send your drawing to sales@yudapcb.com.

Q25: How do you ensure plating quality for HDI microvias and stacked vias? +

We use controlled laser drilling, desmear and electroless copper processes, followed by optimized electrolytic plating to ensure sufficient copper in microvias and at stacked interfaces. We may perform cross‑section checks and reliability test coupons as needed to verify plating integrity and long‑term reliability.

For HDI plating and reliability questions, contact sales@yudapcb.com.

3. High-Frequency PCB

Q41: Which high-frequency materials can you work with and how should I choose? +

We can work with common high‑frequency materials such as Rogers and other low‑loss laminates, as well as high‑Tg FR‑4 and hybrid combinations. The choice depends on your operating frequency, insertion loss target, cost budget and environmental conditions. Tell us your requirements and we can recommend suitable materials.

For material suggestions, email sales@yudapcb
